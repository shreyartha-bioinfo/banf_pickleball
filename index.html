How Payouts Work

Multipliers

Each wager pays out at: wager × the player's final multiplier.

  • Table stage: a player finishing 9th–16th doesn't qualify — that wager is lost (0×). The top 8 secure a base multiplier by exact rank: 1st 1.8×2nd 1.7×3rd 1.6×4th 1.5×5th 1.4×6th 1.3×7th 1.2×8th 1.1×
  • Knockout bonuses (stack on top): semifinal win adds +0.8×; winning the championship adds +1.5×.
  • 💎 Perfect Portfolio: bet on exactly 8 players and predict the qualifiers perfectly — if all 8 finish in the top 8, you get a flat +$50 bonus and +0.5× added to every one of your wagers.
  • Tied ranks: players sharing a table rank both get that rank's (higher) multiplier — e.g. two players tied at 2nd both pay 1.7×.
  • Example: $10 on the player who tops the table (1.8×), wins their semifinal (+0.8×) and the final (+1.5×) → 4.1× → $41.

Quick Reference

Pickleball Rules

  • Serve: Underhand, diagonally cross-court, paddle contact below waist level.
  • Two-bounce rule: The ball must bounce once on each side before volleys are allowed (serve, then return).
  • The Kitchen: The 7 ft non-volley zone at the net — you can't volley the ball while standing in it (or on its line).
  • Scoring (Rally Scoring): Games are typically played to 11, win by 2. Every rally scores a point — for whichever side wins it, serving or returning. Unlike traditional side-out scoring, there's no "free" rally.
  • Doubles serving: Both players on a side get to serve before the side loses the serve — except at the very start of the game (first server only). Serve still rotates the same way; rally scoring only changes who's allowed to score, not who's allowed to serve.
  • Faults: Ball out of bounds, into the net, a volley from the kitchen, or a double bounce ends the rally.
  • Line calls: A ball touching any part of the line is "in."

On Court

Tips for Match Day

  • It's rally scoring, so every point is live — play returns as aggressively as serves, there's no rally to coast through.
  • Stay out of the kitchen unless you're moving in to hit a bounced ball.
  • Third shot drop: soften your return so the opponents can't smash it back.
  • Communicate with your partner — call "mine," "yours," or "switch" early.
  • Return deep to push opponents back from the net.
  • Warm up your wrist and shoulder — pickleball involves a lot of quick, short strokes.
  • Hydrate between games, especially with back-to-back matches on the same court.
