📜 Pickleball Rules — Quick Reference

  • Serve: Underhand, diagonally cross-court, paddle contact below waist level.
  • Two-bounce rule: The ball must bounce once on each side before volleys are allowed (serve, then return).
  • The Kitchen: The 7 ft non-volley zone at the net — you can't volley the ball while standing in it (or on its line).
  • Scoring: Games are typically played to 11, win by 2. Only the serving side can score a point.
  • Doubles serving: Both players on a side get to serve before the side loses the serve — except at the very start of the game (first server only).
  • Faults: Ball out of bounds, into the net, a volley from the kitchen, or a double bounce ends the rally.
  • Line calls: A ball touching any part of the line is "in."

💡 Tips for Match Day

  • Stay out of the kitchen unless you're moving in to hit a bounced ball.
  • Third shot drop: soften your return so the opponents can't smash it back.
  • Communicate with your partner — call "mine," "yours," or "switch" early.
  • Return deep to push opponents back from the net.
  • Warm up your wrist and shoulder — pickleball involves a lot of quick, short strokes.
  • Hydrate between games, especially with back-to-back matches on the same court.
